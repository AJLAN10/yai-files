Shibam — the "Manhattan of the Desert" — stands on a raised alluvial
platform at the floor of Wadi Hadramawt. For sixteen centuries that
platform has lifted the city's 500 mudbrick towers above the seasonal
floods that scour the valley.

## The vulnerability

The 2008 Tropical Storm and the 2020 floods both overtopped sections
of the platform, saturating the `libn` (sun-dried mudbrick) at the base
of several towers. Because mudbrick loses compressive strength rapidly
when saturated, even a single overtopping event threatens collapse of
structures that have stood for 400 years.

## The resilience strategy

The adaptive response follows **[YBC-H06 Flood Resilience](/code)**:

- Verify platform elevation against the 100-year flood level.
- Replace eroded base courses with fired-brick plinths (libn is not
  permitted below the flood line).
- Route all roof drainage to internal cisterns (`birka`) rather than
  discharging onto the platform edge.

The intervention preserves the [Hadramawt mudbrick character](/details)
while quietly hardening the parts of the structure the eye never sees.
