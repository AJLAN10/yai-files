The tower houses of Sana'a Old City already embody a seismic strategy:
horizontal **timber lacing courses** woven through the fired-brick walls
every three to four courses, distributing lateral load the way a modern
ring beam does. The danger is not the historic fabric — it is the
well-meaning retrofit that replaces flexibility with brittle concrete.

## Graded, reversible intervention

Per **[YBC-H05 Seismic Standards](/code)**, any structure over four
storeys requires a seismic survey before restoration, and every
intervention must be reversible:

- External tie rods and timber ring beams — never injection grouting
  of historic rubble masonry.
- Portland-cement injection into historic walls is explicitly
  prohibited.
- Connections must be removable without damaging original material.

The result keeps the [Sana'a Capital character](/details) — the qamaria
fanlights, the gypsum diwan bands, the rooftop mafraj — fully intact
while improving the building's response to the moderate (Zone 2) seismic
hazard of the central highlands.
